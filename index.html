Welcome...

Hello, I'm Holden. This site is dedicated to Organic Design - a design philosophy that I made up as an extension of organic architecture. The principles are essentially the same, adding some of my own ideas. Organic architecture is based on synergy with nature. Forms resemble natural phenomenon, and the whole design is well composed as a whole. David Pearson created the Gaia Charter to better characterize organic architecture.

Gaia Charter:

"Let the design...

  • be inspired by nature and be sustainable, healthy, conserving, and diverse.
  • unfold, like an organism, from the seed within.
  • exist in the "continuous present" and "begin again and
  • follow the flows and be flexible and adaptable.
  • satisfy social, physical, and spiritual needs.
  • 'grow out of the site' and be unique.
  • celebrate the spirit of youth, play and surprise.
  • express the rhythm of music and the power of dance."

via Wikipedia

This charter follows a theme of organic metaphors to outline a synergistic, yet intuitively familiar design.

Why Organic Design?

Form or function? Design or development? Ruby... or C? Most ponder this dichotomy at some point, but is one really greater than the other? Some say one follows the other, or it can go both ways. I would say the difference is in purpose. Form appeals to the intuitive mode of thinking, while function appeals to critical thinking. I didn't just make this up, either. Check out this awesome video on cognitive ease and critical thinking for in depth explanation. Our intuition is honed from many cycles of natural selection, but it can often fail us in areas that require meticulous analysis. Nature is great at spawning beautiful forms that solve functional challenges. Organic design is simply a way of solving design challenges with organic metaphors.

About me

My name is Holden Grissett. I'm from Tulsa, Oklahoma, and currently live in Palm Springs, California. I grew up with a great interest in the natural world. This became my interest in science. Natural science occupied my imagination shaped my creative side. This is what influenced me to make up this design philosophy. I'm aware that much of this is already present in modern design anyway, but I thought it'd be fun to make a page dedicated to it.
